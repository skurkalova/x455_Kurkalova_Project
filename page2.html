Pandemic Madness

Stuck Inside with Nothing to Do

This whole idea started back in January 2021. It was at the height of the pandemic and it was getting scarier to go outside. I had two part-time jobs at the time: swim team coach for the local YMCA and youth art instructor at a local arts center. Both places tried to operate during the first few weeks of the pandemic, but then they closed their practices and classes. I still remember talking to coworkers in the early days, getting increasingly worried about the situation. We had no idea what was coming and it hit us like a bag of bricks. We didn’t have any other job security and the isolation was intense. I felt pretty hopeless and depressed by the time January 2021 rolled around. It felt so surreal.

To boost my spirits, I decided I would pursue a crafting activity. I’ve always loved crafting and I was getting bored with doing nothing in the house. While browsing for some ideas on social media, I noticed a lot of artists playing with polymer clay. I recognized it from my middle school summer camp days, but I had not seen it in a long time. The material seemed easy to understand: it behaved like clay but you could bake it in an oven instead of a traditional kiln. The clay would harden into plastic and hold its form perfectly. I saw artists mold polymer clay into mini figurines, charms, earrings, pendants, and more. It seemed like a lot of fun. After a few days of research, I made up my mind and decided to order some polymer clay.

You can browse information for polymer clay for beginners by clicking this link here.

Starting out, I went with as few tools and materials as possible. I didn’t have much money and I was likely not going to earn any soon, given the circumstances. I saved where I could and invested in tools wisely; a lot of which I still use today like my trusty roller machine. Sitting down at the table one Sunday afternoon, I began my journey with polymer clay. It wasn’t much and my early attempts were pitiful, but I was having fun. I love learning new art tools and it kept me happy and hopeful through the crisis. After baking a few pieces - in which I learned the valuable lesson that baking polymer clay releases a god awful smell when it’s done - I assembled them into jewelry. My mom, older sister, and I shared a jewelry stash in our house. It mostly consisted of tools for repairing jewelry and said broken jewelry, waiting to be repaired. For me, though, they were enough. Within an hour, I had produced my first pair of polymer clay earrings. I was delighted. I still have these earrings to this day and my urge to create more jewelry is still going strong.
